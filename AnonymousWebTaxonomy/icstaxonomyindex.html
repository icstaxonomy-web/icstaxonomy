How to use this taxonomy?

The diagram is fully interactive, so you can explore it by simply hovering over or clicking on any of the nodes. Here is a quick guide:

How to Interact

  • Hover Over a Node: When you move your mouse over any focus area or sub-topic (node), its background color changes (turns yellow) to indicate it is active. At the same time, the connecting edges (the “chains”) light up with a subtle animation. This visual cue shows you which elements are directly related.
  • Click a Node: Clicking on any node triggers a pop-up (an alert) that displays:
    • The ID of the element
    • The Label (or title) of the topic
    • Its Definition
    The definitions are stored in a lookup table (a hash map) within the code, so every node is paired with an explanation that tells you more about what it represents within the ICS taxonomy.

What the Colours Represent

Colour Coding: Different colours on the nodes (for both main focus areas and sub-topics) are used to group related topics visually. For example:

  • Main focus areas (like “FA 1: Governance, Risk, and Compliance”) have their distinct colour palette.
  • Sub-focus areas use varying shades (for instance, warm tones like oranges and yellows versus cooler tones) to differentiate between aspects such as policies, risk assessments, and compliance checks.

These colours help you quickly identify the nature of each topic—whether it is leaning more towards security or safety, for instance.

What the Chains of Edges Represent

Hierarchical Relationships: The edges (or “chains”) that connect the nodes are not random—they show how different parts of the taxonomy are related.

  • Direct Edges: These link a main focus area directly to its sub-topics.
  • Chained (Elbow) Edges: Some edges form a chain (using elbow connectors) that represents a deeper, multi-level relationship. This visually indicates how a broad topic (like a focus area) branches into more detailed sub-topics, which in turn might have further detailed aspects.

In short, the interactive diagram uses hover effects and click events to let you explore each topic and its definition, while the colours and chains of edges provide visual cues about how topics are grouped and interrelated. Enjoy navigating and discovering the structure of the ICS taxonomy!
